THIS WEEK EXPERIENCE

This time the class was all about advanced HTML. I learned about forms, tables, media elements, semantic elements, meta tags, accessibility, and SVG graphics. Among these, I want to talk more about semantic elements. Before this, I didn't know about the importance of semantic elements. For one thing, this helps Google bots understand your page better, and should thus help increase your page’s ranking and relevance according to Google’s algorithms. A lot of it is structural (in terms of page layout and content hierarchy).

Another vital aspect of semantic elements is their role in code sustainability. They act as markers, reminding both developers and collaborators of their intended use. This not only aids in maintaining the code but also facilitates the process of finding and updating specific sections of a codebase.

Additionally, semantic elements provide precision in targeting elements with CSS. Instead of struggling to style generic tags, semantic elements allow for more meaningful and precise styling based on their structural significance within the page.

IMPORTANCE OF WEBSITE’S RESPONSIVENESS

Responsive web design is not a program; instead, it uses only HTML and CSS to make your web page look good on all devices. It is important as it can help solve a lot of problems for the website. It will make our site more mobile-friendly and improve the way it looks on different devices with both large and small screens. With these benefits, it helps increase the amount of time visitors spend on our site and improves the website’s rankings in search engines.

MODERN WEB DESIGN TRENDS

Among the trending modern web design trends, two of them stood out the most to me. The first one is DARK MODE and the second is WHITE SPACE. As for the Dark Mode, on the practical end, they help reduce eye strain, a concern for many as we are spending more and more time looking at screens. On the aesthetic end, dark mode easily creates an ultra-modern look for our website while giving us the ability to highlight other design elements just by darkening the elements that surround it.

The use of white space is about giving content room to breathe, not trying to cram the most information possible on the screen. The experience is more relaxing for our website visitors, the content stands out better, and readability is improved.

White space is just the term for the spacing we give between elements. It does not have to be white, as long as the area is empty.
